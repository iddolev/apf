# Product Requirements Document (PRD) Template

> **Purpose:**
> This document defines the goals, user needs, scope, and requirements for a new product or feature.
> The LLM should interview the user step by step to fill in all sections below.

---

## 1. Overview

### 1.1 Product / Feature Name

> What is the working title or name of the product or feature?

### 1.2 Summary

> Provide a short description of what this product or feature does and why it matters.

### 1.3 Background / Context

> Describe the business or user problem that led to this idea.
> What events, data, or feedback led to proposing this?

### 1.4 Goals and Objectives

> List 2–4 key objectives.
> Example: "Increase user retention," "Reduce support tickets," "Enable new revenue channel."

---

## 2. Target Users and Use Cases

### 2.1 Target Audience

> Who are the primary users? (personas, segments, roles)

### 2.2 User Needs or Pain Points

> What problems are users currently facing that this product will solve?

### 2.3 Key Use Cases / User Scenarios

> Describe 2–3 main use cases or journeys showing how users will interact with the product.

---

## 3. Scope

### 3.1 In Scope

> What features or functionality are included in this release or phase?

### 3.2 Out of Scope

> What is explicitly excluded for now? (helps manage stakeholder expectations)

### 3.3 Assumptions & Dependencies

> What assumptions must hold true?
> Are there any dependencies (APIs, teams, vendors, technologies)?

---

## 4. Requirements

### 4.1 Functional Requirements

> List the specific capabilities the product must have.
> Each requirement can be written as a statement:
> - The system shall [do X].
> - The user can [do Y].
> - When [condition], the system should [result].

### 4.2 Non-Functional Requirements

> Define performance, reliability, scalability, or compliance expectations. Examples:
> - Load time under 2 seconds.
> - Meets GDPR or ADA standards.
> - 99.9% uptime SLA.

---

## 5. Success Metrics

### 5.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

> What metrics define success for this product or feature? (quantitative or qualitative)

### 5.2 Measurement Plan

> How will these metrics be tracked and by whom?

---

## 6. Design & UX

### 6.1 User Flow or Wireframe Reference

> Attach or describe any relevant mockups or flow diagrams.

### 6.2 Experience Goals

> What should the ideal user experience feel like? (e.g., intuitive, fast, delightful)

---

## 7. Technical Considerations

### 7.1 Architecture / Tech Stack

> What technologies, frameworks, or systems will be used?

### 7.2 Integration Points

> Which existing systems or APIs need to integrate with this product?

### 7.3 Security & Privacy

> What data is collected, stored, or shared?
> How is it protected? What compliance requirements apply?

---

## 8. Timeline & Milestones

### 8.1 Release Plan

> What is the high-level timeline, including phases, milestones, or sprints?

### 8.2 Risks and Mitigations

> What are potential risks and how can they be mitigated?

---

## 9. Stakeholders and Roles

### 9.1 Key Stakeholders

> Who is involved or affected (product manager, designer, engineering lead, etc.)?

### 9.2 Approvals

> Who needs to sign off before implementation or release?

---

## 10. Appendices

> Include any supporting materials such as:
> - User research summaries
> - Competitive analysis
> - Related documentation or links

---

**LLM Instruction Note:**
When interviewing a user to fill this PRD:

1. Ask one section at a time in natural conversation.
2. Clarify and iterate until each section is complete.
3. Summarize back what the user said to confirm understanding.
4. Output the filled PRD in Markdown once all sections are gathered.
